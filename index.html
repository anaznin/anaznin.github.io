Food Deserts

Despite New York City being one of the wealthiest cities on the planet, low-income neighborhoods are going hungry and suffering nutritionally. With the rise of industrialized agriculture, modern industrial crops are bred for high yield, ease of transport, cheaper production, and fast growth, rather than for nutrient content. Though sustainably-grown unprocessed food, such as whole grains, legumes, and fresh fruits and vegetables, may be available in middle and upper-class communities, such food is not available to many populations in New York City. There is a direct correlation between a lack of access to healthy foods and health risks, including obesity.
